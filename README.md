# AI Academy Video Generation Prompts (Modules 4-8)

This document contains highly optimized, 5-minute video generation prompts designed specifically for NotebookLM or similar AI podcast/video generation tools. Each prompt is imbued with a "Claude touch"—a focus on empathetic, engaging, narrative-driven learning that bridges theory and practice. 

These prompts strictly align with the AI Academy platform's structure, ensuring the generated content directly prepares students for specific module quizzes and practical interactive challenges.

## 🧠 Module 4: Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

### 1. Problem Identification (`problem-identification`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational video/audio script. **Topic:** Identifying the 'Real' Problem, not just the symptoms. **Tone:** Engaging, 'Claude-esque' empathy, and practical. 
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** Start with the 'Elevator Problem' (people complaining an elevator is too slow; the fix wasn't a faster elevator, but putting mirrors in the lobby to distract them). 
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Explain the difference between 'symptoms' (slow elevator) and the 'root problem' (boredom). Introduce the concept of cognitive biases (like Confirmation Bias) that blind us to real issues. 
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** Speak directly to the student. 'Before your AI Academy challenge today, pause. When you see a drop in sales or a team argument, ask: Is this the disease, or just a cough?'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** Explicitly prep them for their interactive challenge: 'You will face scenarios where you must separate facts from assumptions. Look for the underlying metric, not the emotional reaction. Master this, and you'll ace the upcoming quiz and problem-identification challenge.'"

### 2. Root Cause Analysis (`root-cause-analysis`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational script. **Topic:** Root Cause Analysis using the '5 Whys' framework. **Tone:** Analytical yet accessible, storytelling-heavy.
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** Tell the story of the Jefferson Memorial monument in DC degrading rapidly (Why? Harsh chemicals. Why? Pigeon droppings. Why? Spiders. Why? Midges. Why? Lights on too early. Solution: Turn lights on later!).
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Break down the '5 Whys' technique and the 'Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram'. Explain how complex problems are rarely caused by just one factor—they are systemic.
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** 'We often stop at the first 'Why' because it's comfortable and gives us someone to blame. True critical thinkers keep drilling.'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** 'In your upcoming challenge, you'll be handed a messy business failure. Don't stop at the surface. Map out the branches. If you use the 5 Whys, you'll breeze through the root-cause interactive simulation.'"

### 3. Creative Solutions (`creative-solutions`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational script. **Topic:** Generating Creative Solutions and Lateral Thinking. **Tone:** Inspiring, energetic, boundary-pushing.
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** The story of NASA spending millions on a space pen, while the Russians used a pencil (debunk the myth, but use it to explain lateral thinking—solving a problem by a different approach).
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Teach 'Lateral Thinking' vs. 'Vertical Thinking'. Introduce techniques like SCAMPER (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to another use, Eliminate, Reverse) and 'Worst Idea Brainstorming'.
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** 'Creativity isn't magic; it's just connecting things. To be creative, you need to deliberately cross wires that don't belong together.'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** 'For your next AI Academy challenge, you'll need to brainstorm outside the box. When faced with a constraint, use SCAMPER. The quiz will test your ability to reframe problems—get ready to bend your mind!'"

### 4. Decision Making (`decision-making`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational script. **Topic:** Effective Decision Making under uncertainty. **Tone:** Strategic, calm, structured.
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** Introduce 'Analysis Paralysis'—how having too much data can actually freeze us (the jam study in a supermarket).
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Teach the 'Decision Matrix' (weighted criteria) and the concept of 'Satisficing' (finding a solution that is 'good enough' rather than perfect). Introduce 'Second-Order Thinking' (what happens after what happens?).
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** 'A decision is just a bet on the future. You can't control the outcome, but you can control the process you use to place the bet.'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** 'In your upcoming challenge, you will be forced to make a business choice with incomplete information. Use the Decision Matrix. Weigh your variables. Trust your framework to ace the quiz and challenge.'"

### 5. Implementation Planning (`implementation-planning`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational script. **Topic:** Implementation Planning and Execution. **Tone:** Action-oriented, disciplined, focused.
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** 'A brilliant strategy is useless if you can't tie your shoes.' Discuss the gap between 'knowing' and 'doing'.
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Teach the essentials of a strong implementation plan: SMART goals, resource allocation, and the 'Pre-mortem' technique (imagining the project has failed before it starts, to figure out why).
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** 'Planning isn't about predicting the future perfectly; it's about minimizing the impact of surprises.'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** 'Your next task will ask you to sequence events and allocate resources. Remember the Pre-mortem technique—it will be the key to passing your implementation challenge with flying colors.'"

### 6. Evaluation (`evaluation`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute educational script. **Topic:** Evaluating Outcomes and Continuous Improvement. **Tone:** Reflective, growth-oriented, encouraging.
> **Opening Hook (0:00-1:00):** The concept of 'Black Box Thinking' in aviation—how the industry learns from every single mistake, making flying the safest way to travel.
> **Core Concept (1:00-3:00):** Explain 'After Action Reviews' (AAR). What was supposed to happen? What actually happened? Why was there a difference? Introduce the concept of the Feedback Loop.
> **Practical Application / The 'Claude Touch' (3:00-4:00):** 'Don't judge a decision entirely by its outcome. You can make a good decision and get a bad result, or a bad decision and get lucky. Evaluate the *process*.'
> **Challenge Prep (4:00-5:00):** 'In your final Module 4 challenge, you'll review a completed project. Look for the gaps between expectations and reality. Master the AAR to crush this quiz!'"

## 🎨 Module 5: Design Thinking

### 1. Empathize (`empathize`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** The 'Empathize' phase of Design Thinking. **Tone:** Human-centric, warm, observant.
> **Hook:** Story of the MRI machine for kids (redesigned as a pirate ship adventure because the designer finally talked to terrified children, not just doctors).
> **Core:** Teach active listening, 'Beginner's Mind,' and creating User Personas. Empathy vs. Sympathy in product design.
> **Claude Touch:** 'You aren't your user. You must leave your ego at the door and step into their shoes until their pain feels like your pain.'
> **Prep:** 'In your upcoming challenge, don't jump to solutions. You must first accurately identify the user's hidden frustrations to pass the quiz.'"

### 2. Define (`define`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** The 'Define' phase (Creating Problem Statements). **Tone:** Sharp, clarifying, structured.
> **Hook:** 'If I had an hour to solve a problem, I'd spend 55 minutes thinking about the problem.' (Attributed to Einstein).
> **Core:** Teach how to write a 'POV (Point of View) Statement': User + Need + Insight. Transitioning from observations to a clear, actionable problem statement. 
> **Claude Touch:** 'A well-defined problem is a problem half-solved. If your framing is muddy, your solution will be a mess.'
> **Prep:** 'You will be tested on synthesizing messy user data into a single, sharp problem statement. Nailing the POV statement is your key to the next challenge.'"

### 3. Ideate (`ideate`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** The 'Ideate' phase. **Tone:** Free-flowing, energetic, non-judgmental.
> **Hook:** The origin of the Post-it Note—a failed, weak adhesive that became a billion-dollar idea.
> **Core:** Rules of Brainstorming: Defer judgment, encourage wild ideas, build on the ideas of others (Yes, and...). Teach mind-mapping.
> **Claude Touch:** 'In this phase, quantity breeds quality. Your only enemy right now is your inner critic.'
> **Prep:** 'Your next challenge requires you to push past the obvious first ideas. The quiz will test your understanding of brainstorming rules—remember, no criticizing during ideation!'"

### 4. Prototype (`prototype`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** The 'Prototype' phase. **Tone:** Hands-on, practical, iterative.
> **Hook:** How the first Google Glass or Palm Pilot was prototyped using a block of wood and paper in a pocket.
> **Core:** Explain 'Low-Fidelity' vs. 'High-Fidelity' prototypes. The concept of 'Failing Fast and Cheap.'
> **Claude Touch:** 'A prototype is a physical question you ask the user. It doesn't need to be perfect; it just needs to elicit an answer.'
> **Prep:** 'You'll face scenarios where you must choose the right type of prototype. Don't overinvest time. Choose the fast, cheap option to learn quickly and win the challenge.'"

### 5. Test (`test`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** The 'Test' phase. **Tone:** Objective, observant, scientific.
> **Hook:** A famous product flop that happened because the creators tested the product to validate their ego, not to find flaws.
> **Core:** How to conduct a user test: Show, don't tell. Ask open-ended questions. Watch what they do, not just what they say.
> **Claude Touch:** 'Treat your prototype like a stranger's work. Love the user, not your design. If they fail to use it, the design is wrong, not the user.'
> **Prep:** 'Your challenge will test your ability to gather unbiased feedback. Focus on identifying usability flaws to score 100% on the quiz.'"

### 6. Iterate (`iterate`)
**Prompt for Generator:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Iteration in Design Thinking. **Tone:** Resilient, forward-looking, dynamic.
> **Hook:** How Dyson created 5,127 failed prototypes before perfecting the bagless vacuum. 
> **Core:** Design Thinking is non-linear. How to take testing feedback and cycle back to Empathize, Define, or Ideate. The concept of the MVP (Minimum Viable Product).
> **Claude Touch:** 'Iteration isn't re-doing mistakes; it's updating your map with new terrain data. Every loop makes sharper edges.'
> **Prep:** 'Your final Module 5 challenge asks when to pivot and when to persevere. Understand the non-linear loop, and you will dominate this section.'"

## 🤝 Module 6: Teamwork & Collaboration

### 1. Team Dynamics (`team-dynamics`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Psychological Safety and Team Dynamics. **Tone:** Collaborative, psychological, insightful.
> **Hook:** Google's 'Project Aristotle'—the discovery that the #1 factor in team success isn't IQ, but 'Psychological Safety'.
> **Core:** Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing model (Tuckman). What psychological safety looks like in practice.
> **Claude Touch:** 'A great team isn't a group of people who work together; it's a group of people who trust each other enough to disagree.'
> **Prep:** 'Your challenge will test your ability to spot toxic dynamics. Foster safety in the simulator to pass!'"

### 2. Leadership (`leadership`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Situational Leadership. **Tone:** Empowering, adaptable, authoritative yet humble.
> **Hook:** A captain of a ship steering through a storm vs. calm waters—you can't use the same leadership style for both.
> **Core:** Directing vs. Coaching vs. Supporting vs. Delegating. Servant leadership.
> **Claude Touch:** 'Leadership isn't a title; it's a behavior. True leaders adjust their style to the needs of the individual, not the other way around.'
> **Prep:** 'The challenge will throw different team member scenarios at you. Match your leadership style to their readiness to ace the quiz.'"

### 3. Conflict Management (`conflict-management`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Productive Conflict Resolution. **Tone:** Mediating, calm, strategic.
> **Hook:** Why a team with ZERO conflict is actually a red flag (it means no one cares or feels safe enough to argue).
> **Core:** The Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument (Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating). Focus on 'interest-based' negotiation.
> **Claude Touch:** 'Attack the problem, not the person. Shift the framing from You vs. Me to Us vs. The Problem.'
> **Prep:** 'You will be placed in a simulated dispute. Use collaborative strategies to find the win-win and beat the challenge!'"

### 4. Remote Collaboration (`remote-collaboration`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Mastering Remote Work. **Tone:** Modern, tech-savvy, highly communicative.
> **Hook:** The difference between simply 'working from home' and actually 'collaborating asynchronously'.
> **Core:** Asynchronous vs. Synchronous communication. Over-communication protocols. Building trust without watercooler moments.
> **Claude Touch:** 'In a remote team, transparency is your currency. If it's not documented, it didn't happen.'
> **Prep:** 'Your interactive challenge will test your tool selection and async etiquette. Nail the documentation rules to pass.'"

### 5. Cross-Functional Teams (`cross-functional-teams`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Bridging Silos in Cross-Functional Teams. **Tone:** Unifying, broad-perspective, diplomatic.
> **Hook:** The classic 'Engineers vs. Marketers' battle and how misaligned incentives destroy products.
> **Core:** Aligning KPIs. Translating jargon between departments. Creating shared goals.
> **Claude Touch:** 'Your job is to be the bilingual diplomat. Speak 'data' to the engineers and 'emotion' to marketing, while pointing both to the same horizon.'
> **Prep:** 'You will need to align conflicting department goals in your upcoming task. Master the translation of priorities to succeed!'"

### 6. Team Building (`team-building`)
**Prompt:**
> "Create a 5-minute Script. **Topic:** Building Team Cohesion authentically. **Tone:** Human, warm, practical.
> **Hook:** Why 'mandatory fun' and trust falls often backfire, while true vulnerability builds lasting bonds.
> **Core:** Rituals over forced events. Recognizing contributions. Creating shared identity and purpose. 
> **Claude Touch:** 'Culture isn't what's written on the wall; it's the worst behavior you are willing to tolerate, and the small wins you consistently celebrate.'
> **Prep:** 'In your final Module 6 challenge, you must design a team ritual. Focus on psychological safety and organic connection to ace it.'"

## 📊 Module 7: Data-Driven Decision Making

*(Use the exact same structure for generator prompts)*
*   **1. Data Analysis (`data-analysis`):** Hook on intuition vs data (Moneyball). Core: Types of data (Qual vs Quant), Descriptive vs Diagnostic analytics. Touch: 'Data is the voice of your customer.' Prep: Identify data types in the challenge.
*   **2. Statistical Thinking (`statistical-thinking`):** Hook on 'Correlation vs Causation' (Ice cream sales and shark attacks). Core: Mean, Median, Mode, Standard deviation basics. Touch: 'Don't let outliers lie to you.' Prep: Spot the flawed statistic in the interactive quiz.
*   **3. Data Visualization (`data-visualization`):** Hook on the Challenger Space Shuttle disaster (caused by a poorly designed data chart). Core: Choosing the right chart (Bar vs Line vs Scatter). Tufte's data-ink ratio. Touch: 'A good chart removes thinking; a bad chart requires a manual.' Prep: Select the right chart for the right data set in the challenge.
*   **4. Hypothesis Testing (`hypothesis-testing`):** Hook on A/B testing at tech giants. Core: Formulating a Null hypothesis, significance, Control vs Variant. Touch: 'Guessing is fine, but guessing without a test is gambling.' Prep: Design a valid A/B test in the simulator.
*   **5. Metrics & KPIs (`metrics-kpis`):** Hook on 'Vanity vs Actionable' metrics (Million page views vs Zero sales). Core: Leading vs Lagging indicators. Touch: 'What gets measured gets managed—so be very careful what you measure.' Prep: Filter out vanity metrics in your upcoming business case.
*   **6. Evidence-Based Decisions (`evidence-based-decisions`):** Hook on overcoming Confirmation Bias when the data contradicts your core belief. Core: Synthesizing multiple data streams into a single strategy. Touch: 'Strong opinions, loosely held. Let the data change your mind.' Prep: Defend a business decision using data points to pass the finale.

## 🧘‍♀️ Module 8: Mental & Physical Health

*(Use the exact same structure for generator prompts)*
*   **1. Stress Management (`stress-management`):** Hook on Eustress (good stress) vs Distress. Core: The body's cortisol response. Time-boxing and the Eisenhower Matrix for cognitive load. Touch: 'You cannot pour from an empty cup.' Prep: Triage a stressful inbox scenario in the challenge.
*   **2. Work-Life Balance (`work-life-balance`):** Hook on the myth of 'perfect 50/50 balance' vs 'Work-Life Integration/Seasons'. Core: Setting boundaries, the right to disconnect, digital detoxing. Touch: 'Your boundaries teach others how to treat your time.' Prep: Successfully decline meetings and set boundaries in the simulator.
*   **3. Mental Health Awareness (`mental-health-awareness`):** Hook on destigmatizing burnout. Core: Warning signs of burnout, anxiety vs natural stress, how to check in on a colleague. Touch: 'Checking in on a teammate is a crucial professional skill, not just a personal favor.' Prep: Identify burnout signs in a virtual teammate in the challenge.
*   **4. Physical Wellness (`physical-wellness`):** Hook on the brain-gut connection and how sitting is the new smoking. Core: Ergonomics, sleep hygiene (the foundation of cognitive performance), micro-breaks. Touch: 'Your brain is hardware housed in biology; if you ignore the biology, the software crashes.' Prep: Optimize a daily schedule for energy, not just time.
*   **5. Resilience Building (`resilience-building`):** Hook on post-traumatic growth and the 'rubber band' concept. Core: Cognitive reframing, building a support network, focusing on the internal locus of control. Touch: 'Resilience isn't about never falling; it's about the speed of your recovery.' Prep: Reframe a major project failure in the interactive prompt.
*   **6. Mindfulness Practices (`mindfulness-practices`):** Hook on how top CEOs and athletes use focus training. Core: Box breathing, single-tasking vs multi-tasking (context switching cost), mindful listening. Touch: 'Mindfulness isn't about emptying your mind; it's about choosing where to point the flashlight of your attention.' Prep: Apply focus techniques to complete a complex task amidst virtual distractions.
